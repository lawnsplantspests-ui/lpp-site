What a Community Water Program Covers

One vendor, one schedule, one number for the board to call. Programs are built around what your community actually has — a showpiece pond, a working stormwater basin, or both.

Algae & Aquatic Weed Control

Scheduled treatments through the growing season so the pond never reaches the "someone posted a photo in the community Facebook group" stage. Species-identified, staged to protect fish, permitted when required. See our algae and pond weed pages for the details.

Mosquito Larvae Treatment

Standing water is a mosquito nursery, and residents blame the pond whether it's guilty or not. Bti and methoprene larvicides — safe for fish, frogs, and dragonflies — knock mosquitoes out at the source. Pairs with our community mosquito programs.

Retention & Detention Basin Maintenance

Stormwater basins aren't ponds — they're infrastructure with a job to do. Vegetation management on embankments and bottoms, control of woody growth and cattails around inlets and outfall structures, and treatment of nuisance standing water. Overgrown basins drain poorly, breed mosquitoes, and draw attention from township stormwater inspections.

Muck & Nutrient Management

Community ponds age fast — goose droppings, fertilized lawns draining in, years of leaf litter. Beneficial-bacteria muck programs and practical nutrient recommendations slow the decline and reduce how much treatment each season needs.

Wildlife Issues

Geese fouling the walking path, muskrats tunneling the embankment, snakes in the pump house — as a PA-certified NWCO we handle the wildlife side too, legally and humanely. Most pond vendors can't.

Board-Friendly Paperwork

Certificates of insurance, written proposals with per-visit pricing, treatment records after every application, and DEP permits handled in our name. Everything a property manager needs to file and forget.

HOA & Basin FAQ

What does a seasonal program cost?
It depends on acreage, condition, and how many visits the season needs — which is why proposals start with a free site visit. Boards get written per-visit pricing they can budget against, not an open-ended retainer.
Our basin is overgrown with cattails and small trees. Is that actually a problem?
Yes — woody growth on embankments compromises the structure, and vegetation around outlet structures slows drainage, which is exactly what the basin exists to provide. It's also what township stormwater inspections flag. Cheaper to maintain than to remediate after a violation letter.
Residents are worried about chemicals near the walking trail. What do you tell them?
The truth: products are applied at label rates by a licensed applicator, aquatic products are chosen specifically for use in water, and mosquito larvicides like Bti are bacteria-based and safe for fish, pets, and kids' stray soccer balls. We're happy to supply a plain-English treatment summary the board can share.
Can you start mid-season if our current vendor isn't working out?
Yes. We'll assess where the pond or basin stands, tell you honestly what's recoverable this season, and build the program from there.
