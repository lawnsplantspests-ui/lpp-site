What We Do for Mechanicsburg Lawns

Soil-First Assessment

We start with a soil test on any new-construction property. Most builder lawns are pH 5.0–5.5 and starving — fertilizing without amending pH is a waste of money. Soil sample takes 20 minutes; results back in 10–14 days.

Fertilization Program

Five-step granular fertilization rotation tuned to the season — early spring boost, late spring balanced feed, summer protection, fall recovery, late fall winterizer. Slow-release nitrogen so we don't push surge growth that the roots can't support.

Pre-Emergent & Broadleaf Weed Control

Split-application crabgrass pre-emergent timed to soil temperature, plus spot treatment for dandelion, clover, plantain, and the chronic ground ivy that comes off the woodline buffers in newer subdivisions.

Grub Prevention

Single preventive application in June. Stops Japanese beetle, masked chafer, and June bug larvae before they kill root systems. Critical in Mechanicsburg — grubs are the #1 reason lawns die in late summer here.

Frequently Asked Questions

My new-construction lawn looked great for one year and now it's dying — what happened?
Builder-grade hydroseed is usually quick-establishing perennial rye that thins out within 2–3 seasons. Add compacted clay subsoil with thin topsoil and you have a lawn that can't sustain itself. The solution is core aeration, overseeding with turf-type tall fescue, and a real fertilization program — not more water and more fertilizer on the same dying lawn.
