Our Lawn Care Services

Traditional Lawn Care Programs

Multi-step seasonal programs built around the needs of Central PA cool-season grasses (tall fescue, Kentucky bluegrass, perennial ryegrass). Includes spring pre-emergent for crabgrass, balanced fertilization scheduled to growing patterns, broadleaf weed control, and fall feeding to build root reserves.

Fully Organic Lawn Care Programs

A complete organic-only program using natural fertilizers, corn gluten meal as a natural pre-emergent, and soil-biology-focused approaches. If you have kids, pets, well water, backyard bees, or a pollinator garden, this is the program for you.

Lawn Fertilization

Properly timed fertilization using slow-release nitrogen formulations matched to seasonal needs. We don't over-feed — which most chain programs do and which causes more harm than good in summer heat.

Weed Control

Targeted control for dandelions, clover, plantain, ground ivy, wild violet, chickweed, crabgrass, foxtail, nutsedge, and other tough invaders. Spot treatments and selective herbicides instead of blanket spraying.

Landscape Bed Weed Control

If you want the weeds in your mulched beds and foundation plantings handled at the same time as your lawn, we treat those too — pre-emergent in spring plus selective post-emergent through the season. Details on the Plant Health Care page.

Crabgrass Pre-Emergent Treatments

Crabgrass is the #1 lawn weed in Central PA, and once it's up you can't kill it without damaging your lawn. We watch soil temps closely each spring and time applications precisely — typically late March to early April in the Harrisburg area.

Lawn Grub Control

White grubs feed on grass roots and cause irregular brown patches. Preventive treatments applied in June–July stop the population before visible damage. We also do curative treatments for active infestations identified by lifting damaged turf.

Beneficial Nematodes (Biological Grub & Flea Control)

Microscopic predatory nematodes applied as a soil drench — they actively hunt and kill grubs, sod webworm larvae, fleas in the lawn, and cutworms without any synthetic insecticide. Pollinator-safe, pet-safe, kid-safe, earthworm-safe. This is the grub-control option in our fully organic program, and it's also available as a standalone treatment for homeowners who want a biological alternative to the conventional preventive. Timing and soil moisture matter — we handle both.

Core Aeration and Overseeding

Central PA's clay soils compact heavily. Core aeration pulls plugs of soil out, opening up the soil profile for roots to breathe. We pair aeration with overseeding using cool-season varieties suited to our climate. We schedule core aeration and overseeding only during the first weeks of September — the optimal Central PA window for soil temperature, moisture, and root establishment before winter. Bookings fill by Labor Day every year.

Soil Testing and Soil Health Programs

We pull professional soil samples and send them to the Penn State Agricultural Analytical Services Lab. We get back pH, organic matter content, and nutrient levels, then build a soil amendment plan around what your specific lawn actually needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you require a contract?
No. Every visit is billed individually. Most clients stay on a full seasonal program because it works, but you can cancel any time, skip a treatment, or change programs.
Is the organic program really 100% organic?
Yes. The organic program uses only natural inputs — OMRI-listed fertilizers, corn gluten meal as pre-emergent, compost-based soil amendments. No synthetic herbicides, no synthetic fertilizers, no synthetic fungicides.
How long until I see results?
Weed dieback within 7–14 days of herbicide application, visible greening from fertilization within 1–2 weeks. Real lawn improvement — thicker turf, fewer weeds — develops over a full season.
How is your service different from the national lawn-care chains?
The same person walks your property, identifies what's going on, and treats accordingly. We adjust products to your specific lawn and local weather. We also actually offer a real organic option, which most chains don't.
When should I start a lawn care program?
Ideally in early spring — March or early April — so we can apply crabgrass pre-emergent before soil temperatures rise. But you can start any time.
