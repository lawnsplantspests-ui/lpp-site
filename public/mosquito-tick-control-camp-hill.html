Why Camp Hill Yards Are Different

Camp Hill's borough lots aren't large — but they're loaded with mature oaks, maples, dense ornamentals, and decades-old foundation plantings. That mature canopy holds humidity all day, which is exactly what adult mosquitoes need to rest between feeding cycles. Properties on Walnut, Market, and Chestnut Streets back into shaded gardens that can be 5–10 degrees cooler than the open lawn — and they're the daytime mosquito hideout.

At the south end, the Yellow Breeches Creek defines the borough boundary and brings a different problem: cool, wet floodplain that breeds floodwater mosquitoes after every heavy rain, plus deer ticks moving in from the wooded creek buffer. Properties from Walden Way south get both pressures combined.

Frequently Asked Questions

My Camp Hill lot is small — do I really need a mosquito program?
If you're shaded, you're a mosquito magnet — lot size doesn't change that. A small Camp Hill lot with mature trees can have more adult mosquito pressure than a half-acre Hampden Township lot in full sun. The good news is small lots cost less to treat.
My neighbor doesn't treat — does that ruin the result?
It cuts into the effect of yard barrier alone, which is why we lean on In2Care stations for tight Camp Hill lots. The stations specifically address mosquitoes flying in from outside the treated zone — they pick up larvicide and carry it back to where they're breeding, even if that's two yards over.
Will treatments hurt my pollinator garden or bird-friendly plantings?
Not when applied correctly. We avoid flowering plants, apply early morning or evening when bees aren't active, and EcoGuard Plus is fully pollinator-safe. We're a working beekeeper — pollinator protection is non-negotiable for us, not a marketing layer.
How fast can you respond to a sudden mosquito problem?
Most weeks we can be at a Camp Hill property within 2–4 business days for a first visit. For special events, give us 5+ days of lead time if possible — but call anyway if it's last-minute, we can sometimes work it in.
