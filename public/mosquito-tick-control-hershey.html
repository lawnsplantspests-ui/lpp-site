Why Hershey Properties Get Hit Hard

Hershey sits in one of the worst mosquito and tick corridors in Dauphin County. Spring Creek and the Swatara feed wet, low-lying pockets across Derry Township. The wooded estate lots along Park Avenue, Areba Avenue, and the Hersheypark perimeter hold deer year-round — and where deer live, blacklegged ticks live. Dauphin County reports Lyme disease cases every year, and West Nile virus shows up in local mosquito samples each summer.

If your property backs up to woods, sits near a creek, or has dense ornamental beds and shaded gardens, you're in the high-pressure zone. We treat those properties weekly through the summer.

What We Do for Hershey Yards

Yard Barrier Treatments

Targeted application to the resting zones where mosquitoes hide during the day — undersides of ornamental shrubs, shaded mulch beds, and dense plantings around Hershey's mature landscapes. Reapply every 3–4 weeks.

In2Care Stations

Bait stations placed around the property. Female mosquitoes contact a slow-acting larvicide and carry it to hidden breeding water — including the inevitable kid pool, dripping AC condensate line, or clogged gutter you didn't know about.

EcoGuard Plus Organic

Fully organic, EPA-exempt essential-oil barrier with 30-day repellency. Real organic control for Hershey families that don't want synthetics — especially around playsets, dog runs, and vegetable gardens.

Deer-Tick Perimeter Work

For properties on the Hersheypark wood edge or near Derry Township deer corridors. We treat the leaf litter, lawn-woodline transition, and animal trails where blacklegged ticks "quest" for hosts.

Breeding Site Audit

We walk your property looking for the standing water you've stopped seeing — saucers under planters, low spots in the lawn, tarps on patio furniture, koi ponds without circulation. Most yards have 3–5 sources nobody noticed.

Event Treatments

My property borders Hersheypark woods — is tick pressure really worse?
Yes. The Hersheypark wood edge holds a high resident deer population, which means a stable population of blacklegged (deer) ticks. For those properties we always recommend perimeter tick work in addition to mosquito barrier — it's a different treatment in different zones of the yard.

Not when applied correctly.
