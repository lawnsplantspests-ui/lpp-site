What Annville Properties Deal With

Annville sits in the heart of the Lebanon Valley with Lebanon Valley College at its center and surrounding farmland out toward Cleona and Palmyra. The mix of college-town housing, established borough lots, and rural-edge properties creates a wide range of pest pressures. That mix creates a specific set of pest pressures:

  • Spotted lanternfly on tree of heaven and maples throughout the borough
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in older porch and soffit voids
  • Mosquitoes on properties bordering the Quittapahilla Creek corridor
  • Deer browse on arborvitae and yews — common in subdivisions near preserved farmland
  • Agricultural pest pressure on surrounding farms
  • Nuisance wildlife — groundhogs, raccoons, skunks — on rural-edge properties
