What Camp Hill Properties Deal With

Camp Hill is one of the older suburbs on the West Shore — established neighborhoods with mature shade trees, tight lots, and a mix of pre-war homes and mid-century construction. That mix creates a specific set of pressures:

  • Spotted lanternfly on the silver maples and tree of heaven that line older streets
  • Carpenter bees on cedar fascia and porch trim common in older West Shore homes
  • Yellow jacket nests in old porches, soffit voids, and retaining walls in established neighborhoods
  • Lawn fungal disease on the irrigated, shaded lawns common around Negley Park and Willow Park
  • Squirrels and bats in attics — older homes with gable vents and chimney chases
  • Tick pressure for properties bordering the Conodoguinet Creek corridor
