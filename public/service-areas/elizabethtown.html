What Elizabethtown Properties Deal With

Elizabethtown sits at the northwest corner of Lancaster County with a mix of historic borough lots, college-town housing around Elizabethtown College, suburban subdivisions, and significant surrounding farmland. The pressures range from urban to agricultural:

  • Spotted lanternfly — Lancaster County has had heavy lanternfly pressure for years; tree of heaven and maples throughout E-town
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in older borough homes — porch voids, soffits, retaining walls
  • Agricultural pest work — fly control for cattle, poultry mite and louse treatments, varroa mite management for area beekeepers
  • Mosquitoes on properties bordering the Conoy Creek and Conewago Creek corridors
  • Aquatic vegetation on farm ponds throughout the surrounding area
  • Deer browse on arborvitae and yews — especially properties bordering preserved farmland
  • Nuisance wildlife — groundhogs, raccoons, skunks — common on rural-edge properties
