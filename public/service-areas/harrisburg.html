What Harrisburg Properties Deal With

Harrisburg is Pennsylvania's state capital and the urban anchor of Central PA. From the historic Allison Hill rowhomes and Midtown brick streets to the post-war neighborhoods of Susquehanna Township and the newer suburban-style developments out toward Linglestown, every part of the city has its own pest pressures. That mix creates a specific set of pest pressures:

  • Spotted lanternfly across the city — heavy along rail corridors, Cameron Street, and tree-lined streets with maples and tree of heaven
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in soffits and porch voids of older Allison Hill and Midtown homes
  • Mosquitoes near the Paxton Creek and Susquehanna riverfront — heavy late summer pressure
  • Carpenter ants and structural pests in historic homes
  • Urban wildlife — raccoons, squirrels, bats, opossums — frequent in older city homes
  • Lawn fungal pressure on irrigated yards through humid Central PA summers
