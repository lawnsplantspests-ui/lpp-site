What Lebanon Properties Deal With

Lebanon County mixes the city of Lebanon itself with surrounding boroughs (Cleona, Annville, Palmyra) and significant farmland out toward Myerstown, Jonestown, and Fredericksburg. The pressures range from city to farm:

  • Spotted lanternfly on tree of heaven, maples, and walnuts throughout Lebanon County
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in older city homes — porch voids, soffits, attic spaces
  • Agricultural pest work — fly control for cattle, poultry mite and louse treatments, varroa mite management for area beekeepers
  • Mosquitoes on properties along the Quittapahilla Creek and Swatara Creek corridors
  • Deer browse on arborvitae and yews — common in subdivisions bordering preserved land
  • Aquatic vegetation on farm ponds across Lebanon County
  • Nuisance wildlife — groundhogs, raccoons, skunks, bats — especially on rural-edge properties
