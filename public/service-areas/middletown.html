What Middletown Properties Deal With

Middletown sits at the confluence of the Susquehanna and Swatara rivers — a mix of historic borough lots, Penn State Harrisburg campus housing, suburban subdivisions, and the riverfront properties bordering Three Mile Island and the airport. River-influenced pest pressures are common:

  • Mosquitoes — heavy pressure for properties near the Susquehanna and Swatara confluence
  • Spotted lanternfly on tree of heaven along the Route 230 corridor and rail lines
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in older borough soffits and porch voids
  • Bats in old building chases — frequent NWCO callouts in older Middletown homes
  • Spiders — heavy river/creek pressure including some less common species
  • Ticks on properties near the riverfront brush corridors
  • Carpenter ants in moisture-damaged trim near older homes
