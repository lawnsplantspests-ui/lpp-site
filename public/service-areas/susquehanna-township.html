What Susquehanna Township Properties Deal With

Susquehanna Township is the large suburban township just north of Harrisburg, covering everything from the Wildwood Park edges and Riverside neighborhoods to Progress and the eastern stretches near Colonial Park. The township's mix of established neighborhoods, parkland edges, and newer construction drives a wide range of pest pressures. That mix creates a specific set of pest pressures:

  • Spotted lanternfly on maples and tree of heaven throughout the township
  • Yellow jackets and bald-faced hornets in late summer, especially in older Riverside and Wildwood-edge neighborhoods
  • Mosquitoes and ticks for properties bordering Wildwood Park, Boas Park, and the Susquehanna riverfront
  • Deer browse on arborvitae and yews near parkland edges
  • Lawn fungal pressure on irrigated yards through humid summers
  • Nuisance wildlife — raccoons, squirrels, bats, groundhogs — common across the township
